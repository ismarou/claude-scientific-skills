# Horizon Europe: Programme Structure and Navigation

## Programme Overview

Horizon Europe is the European Union's flagship research and innovation funding programme, running from 2021 to 2027 with a budget of approximately EUR 95.5 billion. It succeeds Horizon 2020 and represents the largest multinational research funding programme in the world.

### Strategic Objectives

1. **Strengthen the EU's scientific and technological bases**
2. **Boost Europe's innovation capacity, competitiveness, and jobs**
3. **Deliver on citizens' priorities and sustain socio-economic model**
4. **Support EU policies through evidence-based policymaking**

### Legal Framework

- **Horizon Europe Regulation** (EU) 2021/695
- **Specific Programme Decision** (EU) 2021/764
- **Rules for Participation** (EU) 2021/695, Title III
- **Model Grant Agreement** (MGA) and Annotated MGA (AGA)

## Three-Pillar Structure

### Pillar I: Excellent Science (~EUR 25 billion)

**Goal**: Strengthen and extend the excellence of the EU's science base

#### European Research Council (ERC) - EUR 16 billion
- Frontier research by individual investigators
- Excellence as sole criterion
- Bottom-up: researcher defines the topic
- High-risk, high-gain research

**Grant Types:**
| Type | Career Stage | Maximum Grant | Duration |
|------|-------------|---------------|----------|
| Starting (StG) | 2-7 years post-PhD | EUR 1.5M | 5 years |
| Consolidator (CoG) | 7-12 years post-PhD | EUR 2M | 5 years |
| Advanced (AdG) | Established leaders | EUR 2.5M | 5 years |
| Synergy (SyG) | 2-4 PIs | EUR 10M | 6 years |
| Proof of Concept (PoC) | ERC grantees | EUR 150K | 18 months |

#### Marie Sk≈Çodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) - EUR 6.6 billion
- Mobility and training across borders and sectors
- Unit cost funding model
- Career development focus

**Action Types:**
| Type | Description | Funding |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Postdoctoral Fellowships | Individual researchers | Unit costs ~EUR 5,080/month |
| Doctoral Networks | PhD training networks | Unit costs per researcher |
| Staff Exchanges | Secondments between sectors | Unit costs per month |
| COFUND | Co-funding of programmes | 50% of eligible costs |

#### Research Infrastructures - EUR 2.4 billion
- Pan-European research infrastructure access
- Integrating Activities and INFRADEV
- European Open Science Cloud (EOSC)

---

### Pillar II: Global Challenges and European Industrial Competitiveness (~EUR 53.5 billion)

**Goal**: Address societal challenges and support industrial competitiveness

#### Six Thematic Clusters

**Cluster 1: Health** - EUR 8.2 billion
- Health throughout the life course
- Environmental and social health determinants
- Non-communicable and rare diseases
- Infectious diseases and pandemic preparedness
- Health care systems and digital health

**Cluster 2: Culture, Creativity and Inclusive Society** - EUR 2.3 billion
- Democracy and governance
- Cultural heritage and cultural industries
- Social and economic transformations

**Cluster 3: Civil Security for Society** - EUR 1.6 billion
- Disaster-resilient societies
- Cybersecurity
- Border security
- Fighting crime and terrorism

**Cluster 4: Digital, Industry and Space** - EUR 15.3 billion
- Manufacturing technologies
- Key digital technologies
- Artificial intelligence and robotics
- Advanced materials
- Space

**Cluster 5: Climate, Energy and Mobility** - EUR 15.1 billion
- Climate science and solutions
- Energy supply and systems
- Sustainable transport
- Buildings and industrial facilities

**Cluster 6: Food, Bioeconomy, Natural Resources, Agriculture and Environment** - EUR 9 billion
- Biodiversity and natural resources
- Agriculture, forestry and rural areas
- Food systems
- Circular systems

#### Joint Research Centre (JRC)
- Direct actions supporting EU policies
- Scientific technical support to EC
- Non-competitive research

---

### Pillar III: Innovative Europe (~EUR 13.6 billion)

**Goal**: Stimulate breakthrough innovations and scale-up

#### European Innovation Council (EIC) - EUR 10.1 billion

**Pathfinder** (TRL 1-4)
- Early-stage, high-risk research
- Open and Challenge calls
- Up to EUR 3-4M per project

**Transition** (TRL 4-6)
- Lab to market validation
- Up to EUR 2.5M
- Single or small consortium

**Accelerator** (TRL 5-9)
- Scale-up for SMEs/startups
- Grant up to EUR 2.5M + Equity up to EUR 15M
- Blended finance model

#### European Innovation Ecosystems - EUR 0.5 billion
- Connecting innovation actors
- Supporting Regional Innovation Valleys

#### European Institute of Innovation and Technology (EIT) - EUR 3 billion
- Knowledge and Innovation Communities (KICs)
- Education, research, business integration

## Work Programmes and Calls

### Work Programme Structure

Work Programmes are published biennially and contain:
- Destinations (thematic groupings)
- Topics (specific calls)
- Expected outcomes and impacts
- Eligibility conditions
- Evaluation criteria and weightings

### Finding Calls

**Funding & Tenders Portal**: https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/

Navigation path:
1. Go to "Search funding & tenders"
2. Filter by Programme: "Horizon Europe"
3. Filter by Status: "Open" for active calls
4. Filter by Type of Action (RIA, IA, CSA, etc.)
5. Filter by Cluster or Programme Part

### Call Structure

**Call Identifier Example**: HORIZON-CL5-2024-D1-01
- HORIZON: Programme
- CL5: Cluster 5 (Climate, Energy, Mobility)
- 2024: Year
- D1: Destination 1
- 01: Topic number

### Typical Call Information

Each call topic specifies:
- **Expected Outcome**: What the project should achieve
- **Scope**: Detailed description of expected research
- **Type of Action**: RIA, IA, CSA, etc.
- **Budget**: Indicative budget for the topic
- **Number of Projects**: Expected number to fund
- **Deadline**: Submission deadline(s)
- **Specific Conditions**: Page limits, consortium requirements

## Proposal Submission Process

### Two-Stage vs Single-Stage Calls

**Single-Stage Calls** (most common)
- Full proposal submitted at once
- Part A (forms) + Part B (narrative)
- 4-5 month evaluation period

**Two-Stage Calls** (some Pillar II topics)
- Stage 1: Short proposal (outline)
- Stage 2: Full proposal (if invited)
- Reduces effort for unsuccessful applicants

### Submission Steps

1. **Create EU Login account** (formerly ECAS)
2. **Register organization** in Participant Register (PIC number)
3. **Access call** on Funding & Tenders Portal
4. **Complete Part A** (online forms)
5. **Upload Part B** (PDF, following template)
6. **Validate and submit** before deadline

### Part A Components

- Administrative information
- Participant information (PIC, contact details)
- Budget tables (per partner)
- Ethics issues table
- Call-specific questions

### Part B Components

**Standard RIA/IA Structure:**
- Section 1: Excellence (objectives, methodology)
- Section 2: Impact (outcomes, dissemination, exploitation)
- Section 3: Implementation (work plan, consortium, management)

**Page Limits** (check each call):
- RIA/IA: Typically 45 pages total
- CSA: Often shorter
- ERC: B1 (5 pages) + B2 (15 pages)
- MSCA-PF: ~10 pages

## Evaluation Process

### Evaluation Criteria

**Standard Criteria (RIA/IA/CSA):**

| Criterion | Weight | Threshold | Sub-criteria |
|-----------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Excellence | Variable (often 50%) | 4/5 | Objectives, methodology, interdisciplinarity |
| Impact | Variable (often 30%) | 4/5 | Outcomes, dissemination, exploitation |
| Implementation | Variable (often 20%) | 3/5 | Work plan, consortium, resources |

**Overall threshold**: 10/15

### Evaluation Steps

1. **Individual Evaluation**
   - 3-5 independent expert evaluators
   - Each scores all criteria
   - Written comments provided

2. **Consensus Meeting**
   - Evaluators discuss and agree scores
   - Consensus Report produced
   - Ranking established

3. **Panel Review** (if applicable)
   - Cross-topic calibration
   - Budget allocation decisions
   - Final ranking

4. **EC Decision**
   - Grant Agreement Preparation (GAP) invited
   - Reserve list maintained

### Evaluation Timeline

| Stage | Typical Duration |
|-------|------------------|
| Submission to evaluation start | 2-4 weeks |
| Individual evaluation | 2-3 weeks |
| Consensus meeting | 1-2 weeks |
| Panel and ranking | 2-4 weeks |
| Results notification | 5 months from deadline |
| Grant Agreement Preparation | 3-6 months |

## Eligible Countries and Entities

### Automatic Eligibility

**EU Member States** (27): All entities from EU countries

**Associated Countries**: Countries with association agreement
- Currently includes: Norway, Iceland, Switzerland (partial), Israel, UK (partial), and others
- Check current status on F&T Portal

### Third Country Participation

**Eligible for funding** (some calls):
- Low and middle-income countries
- Countries with bilateral S&T agreement

**Eligible but self-funded**:
- High-income third countries (USA, Canada, Australia, Japan, etc.)
- May participate but fund own costs

**Exceptional funding**:
- If essential for project success
- Must be justified in proposal
- Subject to EC approval

### Entity Types

| Code | Type | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| HES | Higher Education | Universities and colleges |
| REC | Research Organization | Non-profit research institutes |
| PRC | Private Company | For-profit entities |
| PUB | Public Body | Government and public agencies |
| OTH | Other | NGOs, international organizations |

## Timeline for Participation

### Ideal Timeline

| Phase | Timing | Activities |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Monitoring | Ongoing | Track Work Programme development |
| Call Opening | T-6 months | Analyze call, start consortium |
| Consortium Building | T-6 to T-3 | Partner search, confirm roles |
| Concept Development | T-3 to T-2 | Develop idea, allocate tasks |
| Proposal Writing | T-2 to T-1 | Draft Part B sections |
| Internal Review | T-1 month | Partner review, revisions |
| Finalization | T-2 weeks | Final edits, compliance check |
| Submission | T-48 hours | Upload and validate |

### Critical Dates

- **Work Programme Publication**: Usually December/January
- **Call Opening**: Various throughout year
- **Deadlines**: Typically 17:00 Brussels time
- **Evaluation Results**: ~5 months after deadline
- **Grant Agreement**: ~8-9 months after deadline
- **Project Start**: Negotiable, typically 1-2 months after GAP

## Tips for Success

### Strategic Choices

1. **Select the right call**: Match your research to call scope exactly
2. **Build early**: Start consortium 6+ months before deadline
3. **Read carefully**: Every word in call text matters
4. **Use NCPs**: Free expert review available

### Proposal Quality

1. **Address all criteria**: Don't leave gaps in evaluation
2. **Be specific**: Quantify objectives and impacts
3. **Show European value**: Why EU funding is needed
4. **Visual presentation**: Diagrams, tables, clear structure

### Consortium Dynamics

1. **Balanced partnership**: Sectors, geography, expertise
2. **Clear roles**: Each partner has defined contribution
3. **Prior collaboration**: Track record helps
4. **Coordinator capacity**: Must manage multi-million project

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Wrong call selection**: Project doesn't fit scope
2. **Weak impact**: No clear pathway to outcomes
3. **Unbalanced consortium**: Dominated by one country/sector
4. **Late submission**: Portal issues at deadline
5. **Budget mismatch**: Resources don't support activities
