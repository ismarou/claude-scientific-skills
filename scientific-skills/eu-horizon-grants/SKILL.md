# EU Horizon Europe Grant Writing

## Overview

Horizon Europe is the European Union's key funding programme for research and innovation (2021-2027), with a budget of approximately EUR 95.5 billion. This skill provides comprehensive guidance for writing competitive proposals across all major Horizon Europe funding instruments, including collaborative Research and Innovation Actions (RIA), Innovation Actions (IA), European Research Council (ERC) grants, Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA), and European Innovation Council (EIC) funding.

**Critical Principle: EU proposals require demonstrating European added value—why international collaboration is essential and how the research benefits the EU as a whole.** Unlike US grants that focus on individual PI excellence, Horizon Europe emphasizes consortium-based research, cross-border collaboration, and alignment with EU policy priorities.

### Key Differentiators from US Grants

| Aspect | US (NSF/NIH) | EU (Horizon Europe) |
|--------|--------------|---------------------|
| Structure | Specific Aims + Research Strategy | Excellence + Impact + Implementation |
| Team | Single PI or small team | Multi-country consortium (min 3 typically) |
| Budget | Modular or detailed | Strict cost categories + 25% flat indirect |
| Open Science | Encouraged | **Mandatory** |
| Resubmission | Allowed with response | Fresh submission each call |
| Evaluation | Panel + mail review | Remote evaluation + consensus meeting |
| Success rate | 10-25% | 10-15% (varies by call) |
| Indirect costs | Negotiated F&A (40-60%+) | Fixed 25% flat rate |

### Three Pillars of Horizon Europe

**Pillar I: Excellent Science** (~EUR 25 billion)
- European Research Council (ERC): Frontier research by individual investigators
- Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA): Mobility and training
- Research Infrastructures: Pan-European facilities

**Pillar II: Global Challenges & European Industrial Competitiveness** (~EUR 53.5 billion)
- Six thematic clusters addressing societal challenges
- Joint Research Centre activities

**Pillar III: Innovative Europe** (~EUR 13.6 billion)
- European Innovation Council (EIC): Breakthrough innovation
- European Innovation Ecosystems
- European Institute of Innovation and Technology (EIT)

## Core Proposal Components: The Part B Structure

### Standard Part B for RIA/IA (Three Sections)

**Section 1: Excellence** (Weight: typically 50%)
- 1.1 Objectives and ambition
- 1.2 Methodology

**Section 2: Impact** (Weight: typically 30%)
- 2.1 Project results and expected impacts
- 2.2 Measures to maximize impact

**Section 3: Quality and Efficiency of Implementation** (Weight: typically 20%)
- 3.1 Work plan and resources
- 3.2 Capacity of participants and consortium

### Evaluation Thresholds

| Criterion | Maximum Score | Threshold |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Excellence | 5 | 4 |
| Impact | 5 | 4 |
| Implementation | 5 | 3 |
| **Overall** | 15 | 10 |

**Note**: Proposals must exceed ALL thresholds to be funded. A 5/5/5 proposal scoring below threshold on one criterion will be rejected.

## Section 1: Excellence

### 1.1 Objectives and Ambition

**State-of-the-Art Positioning**
- Demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of current research landscape
- Identify specific gaps, limitations, or barriers in existing approaches
- Position your project as addressing these gaps with novel solutions
- Use recent (within 3-5 years) and seminal references

**Objectives Requirements (SMART)**
- **S**pecific: Clear, unambiguous targets
- **M**easurable: Quantifiable success criteria
- **A**chievable: Realistic within project constraints
- **R**elevant: Aligned with call objectives and EU priorities
- **T**ime-bound: Scheduled within project timeline

**Beyond State-of-the-Art Claims**
- Explicitly state what is **new** about your approach
- Use formatting (bold, bullet points) to highlight innovations
- Distinguish incremental improvements from transformative advances
- Avoid vague claims ("innovative," "novel") without specifics

**Interdisciplinary Dimensions**
- Explain how disciplines will be integrated
- Show complementary expertise across consortium
- Describe knowledge exchange mechanisms

**Gender Dimension in Research Content**
- Address sex/gender analysis where relevant to the research
- Explain if not relevant (with justification)
- Note: This is about research content, not team composition

### 1.2 Methodology

**Research Approach**
- Detailed description of methods, techniques, and approaches
- Logical flow from objectives to methodology
- Justification for chosen methods over alternatives
- Risk register with mitigation strategies

**Technology Readiness Level (TRL) Progression**
- State starting TRL and target TRL
- Show clear pathway for TRL advancement
- Justify TRL claims with evidence

| TRL | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| 1 | Basic principles observed |
| 2 | Technology concept formulated |
| 3 | Experimental proof of concept |
| 4 | Technology validated in lab |
| 5 | Technology validated in relevant environment |
| 6 | Technology demonstrated in relevant environment |
| 7 | System prototype demonstration in operational environment |
| 8 | System complete and qualified |
| 9 | Actual system proven in operational environment |

For detailed guidance, refer to `references/excellence_section.md`.

## Section 2: Impact

### 2.1 Project Results and Expected Impacts

**Distinguish Three Levels:**

1. **Project Results** (Outputs)
   - Direct products of project activities
   - Deliverables, prototypes, publications, datasets
   - Tangible, measurable within project lifetime

2. **Project Outcomes** (Medium-term effects)
   - Changes resulting from using project results
   - Uptake by users, stakeholders, industry
   - Observable within 2-5 years after project

3. **Impacts** (Long-term societal/economic change)
   - Broader transformation enabled by outcomes
   - Contribution to EU policy objectives
   - Measurable at 5-10+ year horizon

**Impact Pathway Diagram**
- Visualize the causal chain: Activities → Results → Outcomes → Impacts
- Required or strongly recommended in most calls
- Use clear arrows and logical progression

**Alignment with Expected Impacts**
- Directly reference call text expected impacts
- Show explicit contribution to each expected impact
- Use quantitative targets where possible

### 2.2 Measures to Maximize Impact

**Dissemination Strategy**
- Scientific dissemination: Publications, conferences, repositories
- Public dissemination: Media, outreach events, policy briefs
- Target audiences and channels for each

**Exploitation Strategy**
- Commercialization pathways (licensing, spin-offs, joint ventures)
- IP management plan within consortium
- Market analysis and business case (for IA/EIC)

**Communication Strategy**
- General public engagement activities
- Social media and online presence
- Educational materials and training

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
- Define measurable indicators for each impact dimension
- Set baseline and target values
- Specify measurement methodology and timing

For detailed guidance, refer to `references/impact_section.md`.

## Section 3: Quality and Efficiency of Implementation

### 3.1 Work Plan and Resources

**Work Package Structure**
- WP1: Project Management (typically)
- WP2-n: Technical/Research Work Packages
- Final WP: Dissemination, Exploitation, Communication (often)

**Work Package Components**
- Objectives and description
- Tasks with lead and participant allocation
- Deliverables with type, dissemination level, due date
- Milestones with verification means

**Deliverable Types**
| Code | Type | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| R | Report | Document, publication |
| DEM | Demonstrator | Prototype, pilot |
| DEC | Websites, patent, press | Communication outputs |
| DATA | Data sets, databases | Research data outputs |
| DMP | Data Management Plan | Living document |
| ETHICS | Ethics requirement | Ethics deliverable |
| ORDP | Open Research Data Pilot | FAIR data outputs |
| OTHER | Other | Software, technical diagram |

**Gantt Chart Requirements**
- Show all work packages with duration
- Mark milestones and key deliverables
- Indicate dependencies between WPs
- Readable and clear (not too cluttered)

**PERT Diagram (if required)**
- Show critical path
- Identify dependencies and parallel activities
- Highlight risk points

### 3.2 Capacity of Participants and Consortium

**Consortium Composition**
- Explain why each partner is needed
- Show complementary expertise and resources
- Demonstrate prior collaboration (if applicable)
- Balance across sectors (academia, industry, SME, RTO)
- Geographical balance across EU

**Management Structure**
- Project Coordinator role and responsibilities
- Steering Committee or General Assembly
- Work Package Leaders
- Advisory Board (if applicable)
- Decision-making procedures and conflict resolution

**Resource Allocation**
- Person-months per partner per WP
- Budget allocation justification
- Critical resources and dependencies

For detailed guidance, refer to `references/implementation_section.md`.

## Consortium Building

### Minimum Requirements

**Standard Collaborative Projects (RIA, IA, CSA)**
- Minimum 3 independent legal entities
- From 3 different EU Member States or Associated Countries
- Each entity established in different country

**Exceptions**
- ERC: Single PI (any nationality at EU host)
- MSCA-PF: Single fellow with host institution
- EIC Accelerator: Single company (SME/startup)

### Partner Types

| Type | Description | Typical Role |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| HES | Higher Education | Research, training |
| REC | Research Organization | Research, infrastructure |
| PRC | Private For-Profit | Innovation, exploitation |
| SME | Small/Medium Enterprise | Innovation, market access |
| PUB | Public Body | Policy, regulation |
| OTH | Other | Various |

### Building a Strong Consortium

**Partner Search Resources**
- Funding & Tenders Portal Partner Search
- Enterprise Europe Network
- National Contact Points (NCPs)
- Previous project databases (CORDIS)
- Conference and workshop networking

**Consortium Roles**
- **Coordinator**: Administrative lead, EC liaison, financial management
- **WP Leaders**: Technical leadership of work packages
- **Task Leaders**: Specific task execution
- **Contributors**: Supporting partner roles

**Consortium Agreement**
- Required before Grant Agreement signature
- Covers IP ownership and access rights
- Decision-making procedures
- Liability and confidentiality
- Distribution of funding

For detailed guidance, refer to `references/consortium_building.md`.

## Budget Preparation

### EU Cost Model Overview

**Eligible Costs = Direct Costs + Indirect Costs**

**Indirect Costs** = 25% flat rate on direct costs (excluding subcontracting, financial support to third parties, unit costs including indirect costs)

### Direct Cost Categories

#### A. Personnel Costs

**Calculation Methods:**

1. **Actual Costs Method** (most common)
   ```
   Daily rate = Annual personnel costs / 215 days
   Personnel costs = Daily rate × Days worked on project
   ```

2. **Unit Costs** (for specific programmes like MSCA)
   - Predefined amounts per researcher-month
   - Includes salary, mobility, family allowances

**Personnel Categories:**
- Employees (contracts, permanent staff)
- Natural persons under direct contract
- Seconded personnel
- SME owners and natural persons without salary

**Key Rules:**
- Must work under direct supervision of beneficiary
- Productive days: 215 per year (EU standard)
- Time recording required for all personnel
- Bonus/variable pay allowed if standard practice

#### B. Subcontracting

**When Allowed:**
- Tasks that cannot be performed by beneficiaries
- Limited to specific, well-justified tasks
- Best value for money principle applies

**Limits:**
- Must be pre-approved in budget
- Generally discouraged for core research
- Subject to national procurement rules

**Not Subcontracting:**
- Purchases of goods/services
- Travel and subsistence
- In-kind contributions

#### C. Purchase Costs

**Equipment**
- Depreciation over project lifetime (unless <EUR 5,000 or fully used)
- Full cost if fully used during project
- Proportional to use if shared with other activities

**Other Goods, Works, Services**
- Travel and subsistence (actual costs or per diem)
- Consumables and supplies
- Dissemination costs
- IPR protection costs

#### D. Other Cost Categories

**Financial Support to Third Parties (Cascade Funding)**
- Maximum EUR 60,000 per third party (unless justified)
- Open calls required
- Selection criteria defined in proposal

**Internally Invoiced Goods and Services**
- Must follow usual cost accounting practices
- No profit margin allowed

#### E. Ineligible Costs

- Identifiable indirect taxes (including VAT)
- Duties and interest owed
- Provisions for losses or debts
- Excessive or reckless expenditure
- Costs for return on capital
- Costs incurred during suspension
- Exchange losses

### Funding Rates Summary

| Action Type | For-Profit | Non-Profit | Indirect |
|-------------|------------|------------|----------|
| RIA | 100% | 100% | 25% |
| IA | 70% | 100% | 25% |
| CSA | 100% | 100% | 25% |
| ERC | 100% | 100% | 25% |

For exhaustive budget rules, refer to `references/budget_rules_exhaustive.md`.

## Open Science Requirements

### Mandatory Open Access

**Publications**
- Immediate Open Access required for peer-reviewed publications
- No embargo periods allowed
- Retain sufficient IP rights for compliance
- Deposit in repository (Zenodo, institutional, disciplinary)
- Include funding acknowledgment

**Research Data**
- "As open as possible, as closed as necessary"
- FAIR principles: Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable
- Data Management Plan (DMP) required as deliverable
- Deposit in trusted repository

### Data Management Plan

**Required Content:**
1. Data summary (types, formats, volumes)
2. FAIR data principles application
3. Allocation of resources
4. Data security measures
5. Ethical and legal compliance
6. Data sharing and preservation

**Timing:**
- Initial DMP: Month 6 (typically)
- Updates: At each reporting period
- Final DMP: End of project

### Open Science Practices (Encouraged)

- Open peer review
- Preprints and early sharing
- Open methodologies and protocols
- Citizen science engagement
- Open-source software
- EOSC (European Open Science Cloud) alignment

For detailed guidance, refer to `references/open_science.md`.

## Ethics Requirements

### Ethics Self-Assessment

All proposals require ethics self-assessment addressing:

**Human Participants**
- Informed consent procedures
- Data protection (GDPR compliance)
- Vulnerable groups considerations
- Genetic information handling

**Human Cells/Tissues**
- Source and consent
- Stem cell research restrictions
- Organ/tissue procurement

**Personal Data**
- GDPR compliance
- Data protection officer
- Privacy impact assessment
- Cross-border data transfer

**Animals**
- 3Rs principle (Replace, Reduce, Refine)
- Authorization procedures
- Species and numbers justification

**Third Countries**
- Export control compliance
- Local ethics approvals
- Material transfer agreements

**Dual Use and Misuse**
- Security sensitive research
- Potential for harmful application
- Risk mitigation measures

**AI and Emerging Technologies**
- Algorithmic bias assessment
- Explainability requirements
- Human oversight measures

## ERC Grants

### Grant Types

| Type | Career Stage | Max Funding | Duration |
|------|--------------|-------------|----------|
| Starting Grant (StG) | 2-7 years post-PhD | EUR 1.5M | 5 years |
| Consolidator Grant (CoG) | 7-12 years post-PhD | EUR 2M | 5 years |
| Advanced Grant (AdG) | Track record of 10+ years | EUR 2.5M | 5 years |
| Synergy Grant (SyG) | 2-4 PIs, any career stage | EUR 10M | 6 years |
| Proof of Concept (PoC) | Existing/recent ERC grant | EUR 150K | 18 months |

### ERC Evaluation Criteria

**Excellence is the ONLY criterion** (threshold 4/5 each, 10/15 overall)

1. **Groundbreaking Nature** (50%)
   - Scientific impact of the project
   - Novelty and ambition

2. **Feasibility** (25%)
   - Scientific approach
   - Resources and environment

3. **Principal Investigator** (25%)
   - Research achievements
   - Intellectual capacity and creativity

### Part B Structure (ERC)

**B1: Extended Synopsis** (5 pages, submitted at Step 1)
- State-of-the-art and objectives
- Methodology summary
- Resources summary

**B2: Full Proposal** (15 pages, submitted at Step 2 if invited)
- Detailed scientific proposal
- Resources and budget breakdown
- Curriculum Vitae and track record

For comprehensive ERC guidance, refer to `references/erc_grants.md`.

## EIC Grants

### Funding Instruments

**EIC Pathfinder**
- TRL 1-4 (early stage, high risk)
- Open: up to EUR 3M, 4 years
- Challenges: up to EUR 4M, 4 years
- Consortium (min 3 from 3 countries) or single entity

**EIC Transition**
- TRL 4-6 (lab to market)
- Up to EUR 2.5M, 3 years
- Single entity or small consortium
- Requires prior Pathfinder, ERC, or similar

**EIC Accelerator**
- TRL 5-9 (scale-up and deployment)
- Grant up to EUR 2.5M + Equity up to EUR 15M
- SMEs and small mid-caps only
- Three-step evaluation including interview

### EIC Accelerator Process

**Step 1: Short Application**
- Online form (5 pages equivalent)
- Pitch deck (10 slides)
- Video pitch (3 minutes)
- Remote AI-assisted evaluation

**Step 2: Full Application** (if invited)
- Detailed business plan
- Financial projections
- Team and capability description
- Freedom to operate analysis

**Step 3: Interview** (if shortlisted)
- In-person or remote pitch (10 min)
- Q&A with jury (35 min)
- Decision within weeks

For comprehensive EIC guidance, refer to `references/eic_grants.md`.

## Writing Principles for EU Proposals

### Evaluator-Centric Writing

**Context: Evaluators read 4-5+ proposals during evaluation period**
- Make key messages immediately visible
- Use clear headings matching evaluation criteria
- Bold important statements and conclusions
- Use bullet points for lists of achievements/objectives

### Structure and Formatting

**Use Clear Hierarchy:**
```
1. SECTION HEADING (matching template)
   1.1 Subsection Heading
       1.1.1 Topic heading
       Paragraph text with **bold key points**
       • Bullet points for lists
```

**Visual Elements:**
- Figures with clear captions
- Tables summarizing key information
- Impact pathway diagrams
- Gantt charts for timeline
- Consortium diagrams showing partner roles

### Language and Tone

**Do:**
- Use active voice ("The consortium will develop...")
- Be specific and concrete
- Quantify where possible
- Match call terminology exactly
- Reference call text expected impacts

**Avoid:**
- Vague claims ("innovative," "cutting-edge" without evidence)
- Overly technical jargon (evaluators may be adjacent field)
- Passive constructions ("It is expected that...")
- First person singular ("I will...")—use "we" or institutional subject

### European Added Value

**Always address:**
- Why collaboration across countries is essential
- What cannot be achieved at national level alone
- How the project benefits EU as a whole
- Alignment with EU policy priorities (Green Deal, Digital Decade, etc.)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Conceptual Mistakes

1. **Weak European dimension**: Not explaining why EU funding is needed
2. **Unclear impact pathway**: No logical connection from activities to outcomes
3. **Misaligned objectives**: Not matching call expected impacts
4. **Missing gender dimension**: Not addressing or justifying absence
5. **Inadequate state-of-the-art**: Too superficial or outdated

### Writing Mistakes

1. **Dense paragraphs**: No visual breaks, hard to scan
2. **Missing structure**: Not following template section numbering
3. **No key message highlighting**: Buried important points
4. **Inconsistent terminology**: Different terms for same concept
5. **Vague quantification**: "Several," "various," "significant"

### Technical Mistakes

1. **Unrealistic timeline**: Too ambitious for resources
2. **Budget misalignment**: Budget doesn't match described activities
3. **Missing deliverables**: Work packages without outputs
4. **Undefined milestones**: No verification criteria
5. **Weak risk assessment**: No mitigation strategies

### Strategic Mistakes

1. **Wrong call selection**: Project doesn't fit call scope
2. **Weak consortium balance**: Geographic or sectoral imbalance
3. **Missing key expertise**: Gap in critical capabilities
4. **Coordinator overload**: Too much WP leadership on one partner
5. **Late partner recruitment**: Rushed consortium building

## Workflow Timeline

### Phase 1: Call Analysis & Consortium Building (3-6 months before)

**Activities:**
- Identify appropriate call and topic
- Analyze call text and expected impacts
- Map required expertise to potential partners
- Contact and confirm partner interest
- Draft consortium agreement principles
- Preliminary budget discussions

**Outputs:**
- Selected call and topic
- Confirmed consortium (Letters of Intent)
- Draft partner responsibilities matrix

### Phase 2: Concept Development (2-3 months before)

**Activities:**
- Develop project concept and objectives
- Design work package structure
- Allocate tasks and resources
- Draft impact pathway
- Internal consortium review of concept

**Outputs:**
- Project concept document
- Work package outline
- Person-month allocation draft
- Preliminary budget

### Phase 3: Part B Drafting (6-8 weeks before)

**Activities:**
- Write Excellence section
- Write Impact section
- Develop detailed work plan
- Create Gantt chart and deliverable list
- Draft budget justification
- Prepare consortium description

**Outputs:**
- Complete Part B draft
- Budget forms completed
- Partner descriptions

### Phase 4: Review & Iteration (3-4 weeks before)

**Activities:**
- Internal consortium review
- External expert review (if possible)
- Revise based on feedback
- Ensure consistency across sections
- Check compliance with call requirements

**Outputs:**
- Revised Part B
- Compliance checklist completed
- Final budget approved by all partners

### Phase 5: Finalization & Submission (1-2 weeks before)

**Activities:**
- Final proofreading and formatting
- Check page limits and file sizes
- Upload to Funding & Tenders Portal
- Verify all partner declarations
- Submit 48+ hours before deadline

**Outputs:**
- Submitted proposal
- Submission receipt
- Archived copy of all materials

**Critical**: Never submit at deadline. Portal issues are common.
